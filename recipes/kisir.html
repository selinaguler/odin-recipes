Kisir: Turkish Bulgur Salad Recipe

Description

Kisir is a traditional Turkish salad made with fine bulgur wheat, flavored with tomato paste, olive oil, pomegranate molasses, and plenty of fresh herbs. The bulgur is softened by soaking it in hot water, then mixed with the paste and seasonings until it takes on a rich, slightly tangy taste. Kisir is often enjoyed as a light lunch, meze (appetizer), or as part of a tea-time spread with friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup fine bulgur wheat
  • 1 cup hot water (to soak the bulgur)
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons pomegranate molasses
  • Juice of 1 lemon
  • 1 bunch fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • ½ bunch fresh mint, finely chopped
  • 4–5 green onions, finely chopped
  • 1 small cucumber, finely diced (optional)
  • Salt, black pepper, paprika, cumin — to taste

Steps

  1. Soak the bulgur in hot water until soft, about 10 minutes.
  2. Mix in tomato paste, olive oil, pomegranate molasses, and lemon juice.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, cumin, and paprika to taste.
  4. Stir in parsley, mint, and green onions.
  5. Serve fresh, at room temperature or chilled.

Enjoy!
